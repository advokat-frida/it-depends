Teaching tool · 12-card alpha

IT DEPENDS

The privacy game where changing your mind is evidence the round worked.

Make the call. Then meet the fact you didn’t have. IT DEPENDS deals one proposed data use and one missing fact, records everyone’s choice and the majority before and after, and leaves the useful argument on the table.

How a round works

1

Read the proposal

Anyone can keep the round moving. No dedicated facilitator or rulebook is required.

2

Everyone votes

Each player takes one hidden, numbered turn. After the last vote, every choice and the strict-majority result appear.

3

Reveal and vote again

Turn over one missing fact, repeat the vote, and talk about what changed or held.

One screen · 2–8 players · no score

What this is (and isn’t)

A discussion machine, not a compliance oracle. Every situation is synthetic. Ship, Slow, and Stop are positions the room has to explain, not grades the page awards.

A deliberately small alpha. The mechanics and twelve dual-use cards are playable now. Every card and the shared table use original, object-first After Dark art.

Not legal advice. This helps a room practise asking for the missing fact. It does not decide whether a real processing activity is lawful or safe, and a human stays accountable for the actual call.
